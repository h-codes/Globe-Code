Destination Reviews

Our reviewers Elizabeth, Honey and Jess share their experiences

Singapore

Singapore is a must-visit destination in Asia. This city-state is located in Southeast Asia and has one of the busiest ports in the world. There is so much diversity in its population, cuisine and culture with food dishes reflecting Chinese, Indian, Malaysian and Indonesian influences. This reflects the diversity of activities and locations that you can visit on your holiday. Local dishes are available at the hawker centres around the city, and if you’re lucky you’ll be able to find the famed uncle that sells ice cream sandwiches just outside one of the shopping centres. Don’t forget to visit the modern public gardens, ‘Gardens by the Bay’ and watch the musical lights attraction that typically takes place at 9pm each night - a symphony of musically coordinated lights amongst the starry sky and nature, it’s not something to be missed. Overall, Singapore is perfect for a 3-4 day holiday trip with friends and family. There’s plenty to discover in this small but mighty city-state.

Tuscany

Tuscany is the perfect region of Italy for a holiday full of sightseeing, from museums and churches to markets and the beautiful seaside of Castiglione della Pescaia. There are so many cities to visit such as Florence, Pisa and smaller towns such as San Gimignano which is known for having the most fantastic gelato. Tuscany is full of cultural landmarks and beautiful architecture, such as the Leaning Tower of Pisa, the magnificent Piazza del Duomo and the Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore. Don’t forget to explore the range of delicious food the region has to offer. Tuscany is a great option for a short weekend break making the most of just one city or a longer break exploring all that the region has to offer. There’s so much to enjoy in this beautiful region.

Brussels

Brussels is the perfect good value city break, from a 12 hour day trip where you can make the most of the attractions in the centre of the city to a longer stay where you can explore a range of areas and partake in more sightseeing. In the centre of the city is the Grand Place, this is a breathtaking building which during the christmas period has a light show where this magnificent building becomes the backdrop to a show of a spectacle of sound and light which is something you will never forget. Visiting Brussels at christmas has other bonuses such as the many christmas markets all over the city centre that will have you feeling festive with a brilliant atmosphere. It’s not just Christmas time that Brussels is an exciting time to visit with tourist attractions such as ‘Manneken Pis’, street art throughout the city and Brussels range of chocolate and beer meaning you will be eager to return.
